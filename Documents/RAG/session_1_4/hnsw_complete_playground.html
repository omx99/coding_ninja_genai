ef=1 — What happens
At each insertion, only 1 candidate is remembered at a time. Like a job recruiter who interviews one person and immediately hires them without comparing others.

✗ C1 connects to C4 only (misses that C2 is closer)
Bajaj Finance impact: A customer query might return the 3rd-closest FAQ instead of the 1st — because the graph didn't explore enough during build.
ef=6 — What happens
Every insertion checks all existing nodes. Like a recruiter who interviews every candidate before making a decision.

✓ C1 finds both C2 (6.08) and C4 (6.40) — correct top-2
✓ C3 finds C4 (4.00) and C1 (4.12) — correct top-2

Result: Perfect neighbor quality. Slower build (O(n) per insert), but done once.
Production rule: ChromaDB default is ef_construction=100. For Bajaj Finance 2000 FAQs, use ef=200. Build cost is one-time — search benefits forever.
